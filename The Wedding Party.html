Ladies I would like to introduce the two of you.

Dayna is a tremendous performer and an incredible organizer of artists, which we all know is a rare and powerful talent. More than this really she is an integral part of a very dynamic group of entertainers and event professionals. It's really hard to explain what the Grand Artique is to someone who hasn't seen Frontierville at Lightning in a Bottle or any of the festivals where this group of wildly talented and even more wildly diverse individuals come together to create really unique, tailored and tremendously detailed entertainment environments. It's probably harder to explain their capabilities to someone who has seen them. The scope of their abilities is very hard to describe, even to very flexible thinkers. In the past couple of years I have worked with them on a few events and two weddings and they have expressed an interest in providing services at more weddings, so I urge that the two of you talk.

Pamela is the founder of The Wedding Party Bbidal Show and has been involved in the wedding industry for a long time. She comes to the party from the vendor side having been a wedding officiant before focusing on creating these bridal shows which are filled with a personally selected group of vendors and includes a fashion show. She has gone out of her way (at her own expense at times) to procure and retain great vendors. She is the only wedding show I have kept doing over the years, primarily because she sees each vendor first as a component of the experience of the show rather than an addition source of revenue. We all need to amke a living though, and this causes her additional stress; no doubt, and I have seen her mid-event doing the same thing, matching vendors while managing the show. Her pricing is very fair and these shows are as much a place for you to get acquainted with other vendors as it is a place to market to a very specific and lucrative client type.

so, when I have an opportunity to introduce a matching set of people that I think can do business together I get a funny feeling inside and I think maybe this world isn't a bunch of savages after all.

Neither of you asked for me to do this and likewise neither of you have gotten my permission to be introduced like this but in apology I suggest 

to Dayna: rent a space at an upcoming show, (the next one is the 29th of this month) I know you like more prep time than this : however, if you could assemble a video from Shane and Britt's wedding and put it on loop, grab a couple of the guys and girls, you'd have a great product to display there. (while writing later in this letter I came up with a great idea which could be the fuinniest thing I've ever seen at a Bridal Show) You guys come as the Bridal party, as in, you get hired for a couple that has no friends so they hire an acting troupe to be their Bridal Party to impress their parents and coworkers.  Haha this should be a movie!

to Pamela, get Dayna on your call list and contact her about your upcoming shows, keep in mind she didn't ask me to do this so you know, I don't know be gentle. Their niche is going to be really hard to categorize because they can do it all; AND, in the most interesting ways you never thought of. They are a group of Music, stage and construction professionals who lean in the direction of Vaudeville but can basically do anything. I hope that when I get married, Grand Artique will make all of the arrangements, of course, by that time most of them will probably be in the wedding party including; probably, the Bride; which is just the kind of custom tailored service that they would be great at providing. ** I just thought of a new story for a book! Regardless of my personal feelings about this crew; professionally, they are like the Cirque du Soleil of creative performance.

I hope this introduction turns into a great relationship
